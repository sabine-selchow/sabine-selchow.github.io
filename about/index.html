I am Senior Research Fellow in the ERC-funded project 20th Century International Economic Thinking and the Complex History of Globalisation at the European University Institute, Florence, and concept and methods developer at Human Impact Pathway, a social venture based at LSE Ideas (London School of Economics) and supported by the UN Trust Fund for Human Security.

Before joining EUI in 2020, I was Research Fellow at the University of Sydney, and, prior to that, I was for many years a researcher at LSE Global Governance and in the Civil Society and Human Security Research Unit at the London School of Economics, including as Research Fellow in Mary Kaldor's ERC-funded project "Security in Transition" and in Ulrich Beck's "Methodological Cosmopolitanism: In the Laboratory of Climate Change" (based at LMU, Germany). I have extensive teaching experience, including at the LSE and Sciences Po, Paris.

I am trained in the humanities and social sciences and apply qualitative and quantitative methods. I have a PhD in Government from the LSE.
